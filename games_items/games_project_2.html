The Last Sheriffs
Game Project

Game Play Screen Shots

This oddball game project was a software test project that was intended to emulate and follow software engineering protocols.

The goal was show how some develop utilize and design multiple art, audio, & coding assets to create an entertainment product for educational, exercise, and experimental purposes, all within a very short time frame of about 3 months. From square one to functional state.

Unfortunately, Covid-19 has brought unexpected obstacles that forced the product to develop as merely as potential "proof of concept". Despite Covid-19 increasing our academic workloads, my team managed to fulfill in most of our software engineering goals and provide a satisfactory product to the emulated client.

I'm further impressed that they were willing to understand and attempt to work in the project despite how unfamiliar the programming language and environment was.

The game-play consists of one player that controls the character with the yellow hat.
The objective is to prevent all outlaws from robbing all of the bank's money and from putting you 6 feet to the ground.

Controls for this game as follows:

    Menu Keys
  • W Key: Moves Cursor Up
  • A Key: Moves Cursor Left
  • S Key: Moves Cursor Down
  • D Key: Moves Cursor Right
  • J Key: Select Menu Item
    • Game Play Keys
  • A Key: Move Left
  • D Key: Move Right
  • J Key: Hip Fire
  • R Key: Reload Individual Round
  • K Key: Aim Weapon
  • J+K Keys: Aim Fire
  • M Key: Jump
  • N Key: Tap to Sprint
  • Shift: Crouch
  • P Key: Restart
    • Overall Keys
  • F1: Window Resolution x1
  • F2: Window Resolution x2
  • F3: Window Resolution x3
  • F4 Key: Switches to FullScreen/Windowed
  • ESC Key: Quit to Desktop

Since the game is in a "proof of concept" state, please note the following:

Local Multi-player was planned, but not implemented. Don't start a game with Multi-player

Weapon Switching was planned, but not implemented.

Melee weapons were considered for the scenario of running out of ammunition. If you're out of ammunitions, press P Key or die [in-game].
...and more elements not fully ironed out.

This project is NOT projected to leave in its early stage as there's other computer projects I must attend to instead. Thus, It will be distributed "AS-IS".
